About Meghan Trainor

Meghan Elizabeth Trainor is an American singer-songwriter and television personality. She rose to fame after her releasing her debut single "All About That Base," which reached number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and sold 11 million copies world wide. As of May 2023, Meghan has released five studio albums with the label and has received many awards, one including the 2016 Grammy Award for Best New Artist.

Meghan became intrested in music at a young age. She wrote, recorded and produced three independantly released albums; "Meghan Trainor" (2009), "I'll Sing With You" (2010), and "Only 17" (2010). 2013 was the year Meghan started to produce songs for other artists. Meghan usually makes pop music with doo-wop, blue-eyed soul, R&B, bubble-pop and 1950's pop influences and writes the occasional rock or country song. Meghan's lyrics frequently include themes of womanhood, body image, and personal empowerment. Outside of music, Meghan had voice roles in the animated films; "Smurfs: The Lost Village" (2017) and "Playmobil: The Movie" (2019). She has also served as a judge in television talent shows like "The Four: Battle for Stardom" (2018), "The Voice UK" (2020), and "Australian Idol" (2023). Meghan has also won awards including a Grammy Award, ASCAP Pop Music Awards, and two Billboard Music Awards.
